Peach Cobbler

Description

This peach cobbler is a staple for late summer/early fall peach season. I make it every year with local Colorado peaches from Palisade. The recipe is simple and delicious. All it takes is 8 ingredients!

As Pinch of Yum creator, Lindsay, says, this dessert is "the BEST peach cobbler! Made with fresh peaches, sugar, and a topping that bakes like underbaked cookie dough, with cruchy sugar broiled on top." DROOLING!

Ingredients

Peach Filling

  • 5-6 large fresh yellow peaches, sliced
  • 1/4 cup sugar

Cobbler Topping

  • 1/2 cup salted butter, melted
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1 cup flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • a pinch of salt
  • 1/4 cup turbinado sugar
  • whipped cream or vanilla ice cream for serving

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Smear the bottom of 9x13 pan with butter. Add peaches and sugar, and mix directly in the pan.
  2. Mix the butter, sugar, vanilla, flour, baking powder, and salt until a cookie-dough-like batter forms.
  3. Place scoops of the mixture over the top of the peaches. Spread with a spoon or with your fingers - no need for perfection here.
  4. Bake for 30 minutes. At this point it should be juicy and bubbly around the sides - if not, pour two tablespoons of melted butter over the peaches during the last 5 minutes of baking time.
  5. Sprinkle the top with turbinado sugar. Bake another 10 minutes and then finish with a few minutes under the broiler for a few minutes to get it golden brown and slightly cruchy on top.
